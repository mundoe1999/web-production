Test out Policy and Curriculum

The Computer Science Department offers exceptionally well-prepared students the opportunity to receive academic credit by examination for CSCI 127 (Introduction to Computer Science), CSCI 135 (Analysis & Design 1/C++ Programming) and CSCI 150 (Discrete Structures) by sitting for and successfully completing a comprehensive examination in any or all of the above areas.

Several points concerning the administrative workings of this exam should be made clear:

  • The student will pay no fee for the course and will receive no final letter grade: only "Credit by Exam" will be noted on the student's transcript. By college policy it is not possible to receive a letter grade for such an exam.
  • The exam will be offered several times throughout the academic year, before the end of the following semester's registration.
  • Students may also schedule an individual sitting of an exam at a mutually convenient time between the student and supervising departmental personnel.
Here are more details regarding the examination itself:
  • The student must achieve a grade of 80% (B) or higher in order to receive credit for the exam.
  • Two hours are allowed for the examination for CSCI 135; three hours for exams for CSCI 127 and 135.
  • The exam will cover the entire semester's syllabus
NOTE:
  • NO STUDENT MAY TAKE THE EXAM IF HE OR SHE HAS TAKEN THE COURSE HERE AT HUNTER AND FAILED IT.
  • NO STUDENT MAY TAKE SUCH AN EXAM MORE THAN ONCE FOR ANY ONE COURSE.
 If you have any questions regarding about the next scheduling time, contact the Computer science department.
